About

I am an undergraduate Electrical Engineering student at UC Santa Barbara interested in wireless communications, sensing, localisation and digital signal processing. My current research focuses on AI/ML‑based positioning using wireless digital twins, ray‑traced MIMO channel data and realistic cellular deployments. More broadly, I'm interested in building communication systems that not only transmit data but also localise users, sense their environment and adapt intelligently to real‑world constraints. I also have industry experience in systems engineering and digital design through internships at Northrop Grumman.

Research

Wireless Digital Twins for Cellular MIMO Localization

Undergraduate Researcher, UCSB Wireless Communications and Sensing Lab  ·  Advisor: Prof. Upamanyu Madhow

How accurately can existing cellular infrastructure localize users in realistic environments? I am working on AI/ML‑based positioning using wireless digital twins generated from site‑specific ray tracing. This project studies how multi‑base‑station MIMO channel observations can be used to estimate user position in realistic urban and suburban scenes.

  • Generated synthetic wireless datasets using realistic 3D scenes and ray tracing.
  • Built preprocessing pipelines for multi‑base‑station channel tensors and position labels.
  • Ran model and preprocessing ablations to study localization performance.
